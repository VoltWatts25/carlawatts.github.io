Splatoon 3's Progression:

Release Day to One Year Anniversary










Narrative Text

The topic for this website is the progression of Splatoon 3. I chose this topic as my favorite game series is the Splatoon trilogy, even though I haven't played the first one. Since I've been invested in the series as well as the many tournaments about the game, I wanted to make this website about how the game has changed in the first year of its release. I wanted to keep track of the patch notes to see how much was changed in each update, how many points each team has gotten in the first few splatfests, and see what team has won the most throughout all of the tournaments so far. To collect data for this, I looked through the Inkipedia, the Splatoon version of Wikipedia. Through this I had found all of my information on weapon patch notes, each version's updates, all of the splatfest results, and the information about the many championships. Without overwhelming the audience with information, I chose to simplify it down to the amount of changes per update, how many total wins each championship team gets, and how many points each team got in every splatfest. The conclusions I have made based on the data I've collected are that 3 out of the 4 in-game seasons with the first update of the season have the most changes, the only outlier is the Drizzle Season with the third update having the most changes. For the splatfests, I've concluded that Shiver has won the most splatfests, seven out of the ten events. This heavily overshadows Big Man's two wins and Frye's single win. For the Championships, I've concluded that team Starbust has gotten the most wins out of every other team. The fact that they participated in 3 out of the 4 championships shown in the data.
